Arts & Culture

Works of art in Florence

Florence, the home of the Renaissance and the birthplace of our modern culture, has the best Renaissance art in Europe. After the fall of the Roman Empire, Europe went through the Middle Ages, where advances in learning, commerce, and culture were stagnant for some time. Around this time in Florence, a renaissance began to promote ancient Greek and Roman cultures and swept across Europe. The Medici family, which ruled Florence for generations, founded the foundation for the development of culture and arts by sponsoring great artists(Florence, 2020).

Michelangelo's David statue
The statue of David in Florence was made by Michelangelo. The statue of David was carved by Michelangelo using a huge marble that failed to be carved earlier. Renaissance artists rediscovered the beauty of nature and the human body, expressing the optimistic human future of the new era. The work that ultimately shows this is Michelangelo's David Award, which means a symbol of human victory(Florence, 2020).
Venus de Medici
Venus de Medici is a 2,000-year-old piece of Venus from Greece that is reinterpreted in Roman style(Florence, 2020).
Salone del Paradiso
Salone del Paradiso in the Duomo Museum has placed the original statues of the Florence Cathedral in their original locations(Florence, 2020).
